Below is the programme initiation, development, implementation and operational workflow for the SolarPro Enterprise Solar Programme Management Module. It is structured to support national, regional, community, institutional, residential, agricultural and generation-station programmes while reusing the existing SolarPro design, BOQ, procurement, finance, GIS, project-management, asset and monitoring capabilities.

SOLAR PROGRAMME INITIATION, DEVELOPMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION PHASES
1. Programme Lifecycle Overview

The complete lifecycle should follow this sequence:

Programme Concept
→ Programme Initiation
→ Needs Assessment
→ Feasibility and Business Case
→ Programme Structuring
→ Standard and Generation-Station Design Development
→ Funding and Commercial Structuring
→ Procurement and EPC Packaging
→ Detailed Engineering
→ Mobilisation
→ Construction and Installation
→ Inspection, Testing and Commissioning
→ Handover and Programme Closeout
→ Operations and Maintenance
→ Performance Evaluation and Programme Expansion

The platform should control this process through configurable stage gates, approvals, role assignments, document requirements and programme-level dashboards.

KEY PROGRAMME MANAGEMENT CONTROLS

The SolarPro programme module should enforce the following controls throughout the lifecycle:

No programme proceeds without an approved sponsor.
No beneficiary becomes a project without qualification.
No project is generated without an approved template.
No design is issued without engineering approval.
No procurement package is created without an approved BOQ.
No contractor mobilises without contract approval.
No installation begins without site-readiness approval.
No system is commissioned without required tests.
No asset is handed over without complete documentation.
No operational KPI is reported without a defined data source.
No AI recommendation becomes an approval automatically.
Every material action must be auditable.
Every programme record must be tenant-scoped.
Every programme project must retain traceability to its originating beneficiary and programme template.
Every aggregated procurement quantity must remain traceable to its source project BOQ.

FINAL PROGRAMME OUTCOME

The completed workflow should allow an enterprise customer to start with a national or institutional energy problem and move through programme initiation, beneficiary registration, feasibility, funding, standardisation, design, project generation, procurement, EPC delivery, commissioning, asset handover and long-term performance monitoring within one integrated SolarPro enterprise environment.

The module should provide full traceability from the original programme objective to every beneficiary, project, design, BOQ, procurement item, contract, installed asset, commissioning result and operational performance record.
