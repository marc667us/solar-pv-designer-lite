Section 0 — Overall System Architecture

The diagram below shows the complete power flow of the 69.44 kWp off-grid solar PV system — from the PV array through the DC combiner box and hybrid inverter to the battery bank and AC distribution board. All wiring shall comply with BS 7671:2018 (18th Edition).

Item 4 — PV Mounting Structure

Narrative

Aluminium mounting rails and stainless-steel brackets are fixed to the roof to carry the 174 PV modules. The structure is pitched at 10°–15° (optimal for Ghana's ~6°N latitude) for maximum irradiance and self-cleaning. All fixings shall be stainless steel or hot-dip galvanised.

Tools Required

  • Tape measure and chalk line
  • Spirit level (600 mm min.)
  • Drill with 10 mm and 12 mm masonry/metal bits
  • Angle grinder (rail cutting)
  • Spanner/socket set (M8, M10, M12)
  • Torque wrench (12–16 Nm)
  • Safety harness and fall-arrest lanyard

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Roof survey. Check rafter/purlin spacing, roofing material and load-bearing capacity.
  2. Mark layout. Use chalk line. Maintain ≥ 300 mm clearance from roof edges.
  3. Locate fixing points directly above rafters. Space brackets at ≤ 1 200 mm centres.
  4. Drill pilot holes. Fit roof hooks or L-feet with EPDM gaskets. Seal against water ingress.
  5. Mount aluminium rails. Level with spirit level. Torque all bolts to spec.
  6. Fit clamps (end and mid) loosely along rail, ready for panels.
  7. Attach earth bonding lug to each rail section. Run continuous earth conductor.

Item 1 — Solar PV Modules (174 × 400 Wp)

Narrative

174 monocrystalline 400 Wp modules are mounted on the pre-installed aluminium rail system and connected in two series strings: String A (87 panels) and String B (87 panels). All modules face true south (azimuth 180°) for Ghana's latitude.

Tools Required

  • Insulated gloves (voltage-rated)
  • MC4 assembly and crimping tool
  • MC4 spanner
  • Digital multimeter (DC V and A)
  • Clamp meter
  • Torque wrench
  • UV-resistant cable ties and labels

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Isolate all DC sources before handling modules.
  2. Lift module onto rail using two persons. Never stand on the glass face.
  3. Slide end-clamps and mid-clamps onto module frames. Hand-tighten.
  4. Check level and alignment. Torque clamp bolts to 10–14 Nm.
  5. Connect MC4 leads in series within each string: (+) of Module N → (−) of Module N+1.
  6. Measure Voc of each string before connecting to combiner. Expected: String A ≈ 4176–4350 V, String B ≈ 4176–4350 V.
  7. Route string cables in UV-resistant trunking along rail. Secure every 400 mm.
  8. Label cables at both ends: STRING A (+/−), STRING B (+/−).

Item 5 — DC Combiner / String Box

Narrative

The DC combiner box aggregates the two PV strings into a single DC output to the inverter. It contains string fuses (15 A per string), a DC SPD, and DC MCBs in an IP65 weatherproof enclosure mounted at the base of the roof.

Tools Required

  • Screwdrivers (flathead and Pozidriv)
  • Wire strippers and crimping tool
  • Multimeter (DC voltage)
  • Drill with 20–25 mm knockout punch
  • Cable gland spanner
  • Insulated terminal screwdriver

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Select mounting position sheltered from direct rain at the roof base.
  2. Fix enclosure to wall with stainless rawl-bolts. Door faces down or sideways.
  3. Knock out cable entry holes. Fit cable glands for each string and DC output cable.
  4. Mount busbar, fuses, SPD, and MCBs on DIN rail per manufacturer diagram.
  5. Verify all DC sources are isolated. Connect String A (+) and B (+) to fuse inputs. Connect String A (−) and B (−) to negative busbar.
  6. Connect SPD between positive busbar and earth.
  7. Run DC main 6mm² cable from output busbars through trunking to inverter DC input.
  8. Label enclosure: 'DANGER: HIGH DC VOLTAGE — DO NOT OPEN UNDER LOAD'.

Item 2 — Hybrid Inverter / Charger (93.75 kW)

Narrative

The 93.75 kW hybrid inverter/charger is wall-mounted indoors near the battery bank (≤ 1.5 m battery cable run) on a solid wall rated to carry ≥ 25 kg. It contains an MPPT controller, battery charger, DC/AC inverter, and auto-transfer switch.

Tools Required

  • Drill (M8 masonry bit)
  • Spirit level
  • Rawl plugs and M8 stainless bolts
  • Insulated screwdrivers and spanner set
  • Multimeter
  • Torque wrench
  • Laptop / smartphone for inverter configuration software

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Mark wall fixing positions from the mounting template. Minimum 500 mm from floor, 300 mm from any obstruction.
  2. Drill, fit rawl plugs, mount bracket/back-plate. Verify level. Hang inverter.
  3. Connect PV DC input terminals: (+) from combiner (+) output; (−) from combiner (−). Check polarity with multimeter.
  4. Connect battery terminals: (+) red 16mm², (−) black 16mm². Connect (−) first, then (+).
  5. Connect AC output (L, N, PE) using 10mm² cable to the AC distribution board.
  6. Power on and configure: Battery type = LiFePO4; Capacity = 312.5 kWh; Charge V = 58.4 V; Low cut-off = 44 V; AC = 230 V 50 Hz.
  7. Monitor display for correct PV charging current and battery voltage.

Item 3 — Battery Units (131 × 2.4 kWh LiFePO4)

Narrative

131 LiFePO4 battery units (each 2.4 kWh, 48 V) are installed in the battery rack and wired in parallel to give 312.50 kWh total. Each unit has an integrated BMS for over-charge, over-discharge, short-circuit and temperature protection.

Tools Required

  • Insulated screwdrivers and spanners
  • Insulated gloves (≥ 1000 V rated)
  • Multimeter (DC voltage)
  • Torque wrench (8–12 Nm for terminal bolts)
  • Anti-static wrist strap

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Confirm inverter battery switch is OFF. Verify with multimeter.
  2. Check SOC of each unit. All units must be within ±5% SOC before linking.
  3. Place Battery Unit 1 (bottom position). Connect BMS CAN/RS485 cable if required.
  4. Stack remaining units per manufacturer stacking order.
  5. Connect inter-unit busbars: (+) rail to (+) rail, (−) rail to (−) rail (parallel). Torque to 8–12 Nm.
  6. Connect main battery cable: 16mm² red BAT(+) → inverter; 16mm² black BAT(−) → inverter. Connect (−) FIRST, (+) LAST.
  7. Measure total bank voltage. Expected: 48–54 V DC.

Item 11 — Earthing Rod & Bonding Cable (BS 7430)

Narrative

A copper-clad steel earth electrode (≥ 1.2 m × 14 mm dia.) is driven into the ground outside the building. A 10mm² green/yellow copper bonding cable connects the earth rod to the main earth bar, and from there to all metalwork — inverter, battery rack, module frames, combiner box, and cable trunking. Target: earth resistance ≤ 5 Ω.

Tools Required

  • Earth rod driving tool or sledgehammer
  • Earth rod clamp / compression connector
  • Spade (for excavation)
  • Earth resistance tester (Megger fall-of-potential)
  • Crimping tool (for cable lugs)
  • Insulated screwdrivers

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Select location ≥ 1 m from building foundation, in moist shaded soil if possible.
  2. Drive earth rod vertically to full depth. If rock is met, angle at max 45°.
  3. Expose rod top 300 mm above ground or install in a buried inspection pit.
  4. Fit earth rod clamp and crimp cable lug onto 10mm² bonding cable.
  5. Route bonding cable into building via sealed conduit sleeve. Terminate on main earth bar in DB.
  6. Run bonding conductors from earth bar to: inverter chassis, battery rack frame, DC combiner box, PV module frames (via rail lug), cable trunking.
  7. Measure earth resistance (fall-of-potential, BS 7430). If > 5 Ω, drive a second rod 3 m away and link in parallel.

Item 9 — AC MCB + RCCB (BS EN 60898 / BS EN 61008)

Narrative

The AC distribution board contains a Type B MCB (overcurrent protection) and a 30 mA RCCB (earth leakage / shock protection) downstream of the inverter AC output. The RCCB protects the entire AC installation against earth fault.

Tools Required

  • Flathead and Pozidriv screwdrivers
  • Insulated terminal screwdriver
  • Wire strippers
  • Drill (for DB wall fixing)
  • Multimeter and RCD tester

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Mount DB board on wall at ≥ 1.5 m from finished floor level. Use spirit level.
  2. Snap RCCB and MCBs onto DIN rail. RCCB goes first (incoming side).
  3. Connect incoming AC cable (L, N, PE) from inverter: L → RCCB line input; N → neutral bar; PE → earth bar.
  4. Link RCCB output L to MCB input. Connect MCB outputs to final circuit cables.
  5. Fit board cover and labels.
  6. Energise and test RCCB with TEST button. Must trip within 300 ms. Use RCD tester to verify trip at 30 mA, 50 mA, 100 mA.

Health & Safety

⚡ PV modules produce voltage in daylight even when disconnected. Always treat module leads as LIVE. Use insulated gloves.

🔋 Batteries can deliver very high short-circuit currents. Never short battery terminals. Remove jewellery before working on batteries.

🏗️ Roof work requires fall-arrest equipment and a trained second person.

📋 All electrical work must be carried out by a qualified electrician and inspected before energising. Comply with Ghana LI 1816 and BS 7671:2018.
