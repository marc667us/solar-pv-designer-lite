1
Complete System Single-Line Wiring Diagram
①PV Array → Combiner Box: Each string wired with 6mm² UV-resistant DC solar cable (TÜV certified). Individual string fuses rated at 1.25 × Isc protect against reverse current. DC SPD (Type 2) inside combiner protects against lightning-induced transients.
②DC Isolator: 3-pole DC-rated isolator (≥ 1.25 × Voc) provides safe disconnection of PV array from inverter for maintenance. Must be DC-rated — AC isolators must not be used on DC circuits.
③Hybrid Inverter/Charger: Converts DC from PV and battery to AC mains supply. Built-in MPPT controller optimises PV harvest. Battery charger maintains charge using grid or generator when solar is insufficient.
④Battery Bank: {{ r.num_bat }} × {{ r.unit_bat_kwh|fmt(2) }} kWh compact LiFePO₄ unit{{ 's' if r.num_bat > 1 else '' }} ({{ r.bat_kwh|fmt(1) }} kWh total) connected to inverter battery port. Battery fuse sized at 1.25 × max charge current. Mounted in ventilated, shaded enclosure — minimum 300 mm clearance from walls and ceiling.
⑤AC SPD + Main DB: Type 2 AC SPD on incoming to DB. RCCB (30mA) on incomer protects against earth faults. Individual MCBs protect each final circuit. All per BS 7671:2018 Chapter 41 (Protective Measures).
⑥Earthing System: TT earthing arrangement. Copper earth rod ≥ 2.4m driven vertically, electrode resistance ≤ 10Ω (BS 7430). All metalwork bonded — inverter chassis, battery rack, DB enclosure, panel frames.
{# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ DIAGRAM 2 — PV STRING CONFIGURATION ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}
2
PV Array String Configuration & Roof Layout
①Series Wiring: Panels within each string are connected in series (+ terminal of one panel to − terminal of the next). This multiplies voltage while keeping current the same. String voltage = {{ pps }} panels × ~24V Voc ≈ {{ pps*24 }}V — within MPPT range.
②Parallel Strings: {{ num_strings }} strings are connected in parallel at the combiner box. Parallel connection multiplies current while voltage remains the same. Total array Isc ≈ {{ num_strings }} × 10A = {{ num_strings*10 }}A DC.
③Mounting Clearances: Maintain ≥ 600mm from all roof edges (wind uplift). Allow ≥ 150mm gap under panels for airflow (reduces temperature and improves output). Inter-row shading distance = panel height × tan(elevation angle).
④Orientation: Face panels toward the equator (south in northern hemisphere, north in southern hemisphere). Use region-specific tilt angle from solar data. Avoid shading from 09:00–15:00 (peak generation window).
{# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ DIAGRAM 3 — AC DISTRIBUTION BOARD LAYOUT ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}
3
AC Distribution Board — Internal Arrangement & Circuit Schedule
①RCCB (Residual Current Circuit Breaker): 30mA sensitivity on incoming supply protects against earth faults and electric shock. Type A detects both AC and pulsating DC residual currents. Mandatory per BS 7671 Regulation 415.1.
②MCB Ratings: Each final circuit protected by MCB rated to match cable ampacity. Lighting: 10A / 1.5mm². Sockets: 16A / 2.5mm². Air conditioning: 32A / {{ r.ac_cables[-1].cable_size_mm2 if r.ac_cables else "6" }}mm². Discrimination maintained — incomer trips last.
③AC SPD (Surge Protection Device): Type 2 SPD (BS EN 61643) connected between phase and earth inside the DB. Protects downstream equipment from voltage surges. Connection cable ≤ 0.5m to minimise lead inductance and maximise clamping effectiveness.
{# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ DIAGRAM 4 — PHYSICAL SITE LAYOUT ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}
4
Physical Site Layout — Equipment Placement & Cable Routes
①Equipment Room: House inverter, batteries, and DB in a ventilated, shaded, lockable room. Ambient temperature ≤ 40°C. Minimum 300mm clearance around all equipment for cooling and maintenance access.
②Cable Routing: All DC cables run in metallic conduit or cable tray from roof to equipment room. Segregate DC cables from AC cables (separate conduits or ≥ 50mm separation). Label at both ends and at every junction.
③Battery Installation: Batteries on purpose-built steel rack, bolted to floor or wall. Ensure ventilation (hydrogen gas risk in VRLA; LiFePO₄ has low risk but still ventilate). No ignition sources nearby. Fire extinguisher within 5m.
④Earthing & Bonding: Single earth rod ≥ 2.4m, ≥ 2m from building. Test resistance ≤ 10Ω (BS 7430). Bond all metalwork — panel frames, inverter chassis, battery rack, DB enclosure — to earth bar with minimum 6mm² green/yellow cable.
