PV Panel Wiring Detail — Cell Layout, Bypass Diodes & MC4 Series Connection
①Series connection: Positive (MC4 Male+) of each panel connects to the Negative (MC4 Female−) of the next. Voltages add: {{ pps }} × ~24 V ≈ {{ pps*24 }}V string Voc. Current = single panel Isc (~10 A). Check: string Voc must be below inverter max DC input voltage before connecting.
②Bypass diodes: 3 per panel (one per cell sub-group) in the junction box. During partial shade a cell group is bypassed rather than becoming a hot spot. Rated 15 A / 100 V (Schottky). Failure shows as reduced string Voc — test with DC clamp meter at the combiner box.
③Polarity check: Before connecting any string to the combiner, use a DC-rated multimeter to verify polarity and measure string Voc (expected {{ pps*24 }}–{{ pps*25 }}V). Mark each MC4 cable pair at the combiner end with string number and polarity label. Two-person sign-off required before connection.

Battery Bank Wiring — BMS, Busbars, ANL Fuses & Inverter Connection
①Parallel wiring: All + terminals connect to the DC+ copper busbar; all − terminals to DC− busbar. Voltage stays {{ voltage }}V. Use identical cable lengths from each unit to busbar (within 100mm) to equalise current share. 16mm² minimum busbar.
②ANL fuses: Each unit fused on the positive cable within 150mm of the terminal. Rating = 1.25 × max charge current per unit. Replace only with identical rating. Log any blown fuse — investigate short circuit cause before reconnecting.
③BMS communications: Daisy-chain RS485/CAN cable between all battery units and into the BMS master. The BMS master connects to the inverter BMS port (RS485 or CAN). This tells the inverter the real state-of-charge — without it, the inverter estimates SOC from voltage only (inaccurate).
④Cable termination: 25mm² flex terminated with copper ring lugs, crimped at 6-8 tonnes force. Apply anti-oxidant paste on terminals before torquing. Torque all battery terminal bolts to manufacturer specification (typically 10–15 Nm). Re-torque after first week of operation.

Equipment Arrangement — Front Elevation · Side Elevation · Floor Plan
①Front elevation: Inverter at eye level (centre ~1.4m AFF). Battery rack floor-anchored beside inverter. DB top rail at ≤ 1.8m AFF. All equipment accessible from the front. Maintain minimum 750mm aisle. Anti-tip bracket bolted to wall at top of battery rack.
②Side clearances: ≥ 300mm in front of battery rack for ventilation & maintenance access. ≥ 500mm above top of rack to ceiling. ≥ 50mm between inverter rear and wall. Allow cable bend radius ≥ 6× cable diameter at all corners.
③Floor plan: DC combiner box near roof entry point — keep DC cable run short. Battery cables (< 2m) direct from rack to inverter. AC trunking runs along wall to DB. Earth busbar central and accessible from all equipment. Door minimum 900mm for equipment delivery.
④Ventilation: Low-level vent 150mm² at floor, high-level vent 150mm² on opposite wall, minimum 4 air changes/hour. Target ambient ≤ 40°C. LiFePO₄ produces minimal gas but ventilation mandatory (IEC 62485-3). No ignition sources within 2m of battery rack.

Mixing brands risks incompatible tolerances and loss of wind-load certification. Torque all clamp bolts to specification (typically 20 Nm) and re-check after first storm season.

②DC cable management (buried): All DC string cables must be routed in IP67 armoured conduit buried ≥ 600mm below grade from array to equipment room. Use cable draw-pits every 20m for pulling cables. At array edge, cables exit grade in IP65 rigid conduit with sweep bend and weatherproof gland. Lock all MC4 connectors with MC4 spanners.
{% else %}
②DC cable management: Route DC cables in IP44 metallic conduit from roof to equipment room. Use IP67 cable glands at every enclosure entry. Separate DC and AC conduits by ≥ 50mm. Lock all MC4 connectors with MC4 spanners — finger-tightened connectors arc and cause fires. Cable ties every 200mm on rails.
{% endif %}
③AC containment: Route AC cables in PVC trunking (50×50mm minimum for main run). Use pre-formed corners and junction boxes — do not nick cable insulation with cutter. Cable tray for horizontal equipment room runs. All trunking/tray must be fixed at ≤ 500mm centres. Label trunking lid with circuit references.
④Structural fastenings: Battery rack must be anchored to floor (M12, 80mm embed) AND anti-tip bracket to wall (M10, 4 bolts minimum). Cable lugs crimped at ≥ 6T force — soldering alone is not acceptable. Apply anti-oxidant compound to all copper lug/busbar connections before final torque. Check torque after one week.
