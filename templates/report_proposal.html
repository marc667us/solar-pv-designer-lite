What is Peak Sun Hours (PSH)?
Peak Sun Hours (PSH) is the number of hours per day when sunlight intensity averages 1,000 W/m² — the standard used to measure solar panel output. At {{ d.region }}, the PSH is {{ d.psh }} hours/day. {% if d.psh >= 6 %}This is an Excellent solar resource — among the best globally. {% elif d.psh >= 5 %}This is a Good solar resource, suitable for economical PV. {% else %}This is a Moderate solar resource. System sizing accounts for this. {% endif %}
Why does temperature matter?
Solar panels produce slightly less power when they are hot. At {{ d.avg_temp }}°C average ambient temperature, the panels operate at approximately {{ d.avg_temp + 20 }}°C surface temperature, causing a temperature derating of about {{ ([(d.avg_temp - 25) * 0.35, 0]|max)|round(1) }}% from their rated output. The system has been sized to compensate for this loss.

{# MPPT Charge Controller #}
🎯
MPPT Charge Controller
{{ r.mppt_a if r.mppt_a else "—" }} A · Maximum Power Point Tracking
The MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking) charge controller is built into the hybrid inverter. It continuously monitors the solar panels and adjusts the electrical load to extract the maximum possible power at all times, regardless of changing sunlight conditions. Without MPPT, panels might only deliver 70–80% of their potential output. With MPPT, efficiency improves by 10–30% compared to older PWM controllers, especially on partly cloudy days. It also protects the battery from overcharging and ensures safe, efficient charging.
{{ r.mppt_a if r.mppt_a else "—" }} A rating {{ d.voltage }}V DC bus 10–30% gain vs PWM Overcharge protection
{# DC Combiner / Protection #}
🔌
DC Combiner & Protection
String Fuses · DC Isolator · DC SPD
The DC combiner box connects all solar panel strings together before sending power to the inverter. It contains: String fuses — protect each panel string individually so a fault in one string doesn't affect others; DC Isolator — a safety switch that allows the panels to be safely disconnected from the inverter for maintenance (must be DC-rated — AC switches cannot be used on DC circuits); DC Surge Protection Device (SPD) — protects all equipment from voltage spikes caused by lightning or grid disturbances.
1.25× Isc fuses 3-pole DC isolator Type 2 DC SPD IEC 62305 compliant
{# AC Distribution Board #}
🏠
AC Distribution Board (DB)
RCCB · MCBs · AC SPD · BS 7671
The distribution board (DB or consumer unit) is where AC electricity from the inverter is distributed to all the circuits in the building. RCCB (30mA) — detects earth faults as small as 30 milliamps and trips within 40ms, preventing electric shock — this is the most important personal safety device. MCBs (Miniature Circuit Breakers) — protect each individual circuit (lighting, sockets, air conditioning) from overloads and short circuits. AC SPD — protects sensitive electronics from voltage surges. All designed and sized to BS 7671:2018 (18th Edition) regulations.
RCCB 30mA incomer MCB per circuit Type 2 AC SPD BS 7671:2018
{# Earthing explanation #}
Earthing & Bonding System (BS 7430)
Earthing is one of the most critical safety systems in any electrical installation. A copper earth rod (minimum 2.4m long) is driven into the ground outside the building. This rod provides a low-resistance path for fault currents to safely drain into the earth rather than through a person. All metal parts of the system — solar panel frames, inverter chassis, battery rack, and DB enclosure — are connected to this earth rod using thick green/yellow cable. This is called bonding. Together, earthing and bonding ensure that if any live wire touches any metal part, the protective devices (RCCBs, fuses) immediately disconnect the supply. The earth electrode resistance must be ≤ 10 Ω (tested before energising).

{# Cables #}
A5. AC Cable Sizing (BS 7671)
Why cable sizing matters
Every cable carries an electric current. If the cable is too thin for the current it carries, it will overheat — this is a fire risk. Too thin also causes excess voltage drop (energy lost as heat in the cable), making your appliances run below their rated voltage. All cables in this design are selected so that voltage drop stays below 3% (BS 7671 requirement for final circuits) and the cable is rated for at least 125% of the connected load.

Understanding NPV, IRR, and Payback
Simple Payback — the number of years until your total savings equal your investment. A payback of {{ eco.payback|fmt(1) }} years means the system "pays for itself" and then generates pure profit for the remaining {{ (25 - eco.payback)|int }} years of its life.

NPV (Net Present Value) — the total value created by the investment in today's money, after accounting for inflation and the time value of money at a 12% discount rate. An NPV of {{ d.symbol }} {{ eco.npv|fmt(0) }} means the project creates this much real value above and beyond a 12% return.

IRR (Internal Rate of Return) — the annualised return the investment earns, comparable to a bank interest rate. An IRR of {{ "%.1f"|format(eco.irr_pct) if eco.irr_pct else "N/A" }}% means this solar investment outperforms most savings accounts, bonds, and many equity investments.
